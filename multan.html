Multan

Discover the "City of Saints" in Southern Punjab

About Multan

Multan, known as the "City of Saints," is one of the oldest cities in Pakistan located in southern Punjab. Famous for its Sufi shrines, including the shrine of Shah Rukn-e-Alam, and its distinctive blue pottery. The city sits on the banks of the Chenab River and has a rich history spanning over 7,000 years.

Multan is an important industrial and commercial center, known for its textile production, agriculture, and traditional crafts. The city's adobe architecture and spiritual heritage make it a unique destination. Multan is also known for its distinctive sweets, especially Sohan Halwa.

  • Multan Fort

  • Qasim Bagh

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Multan is from October to March when the weather is cooler. The summer months can be extremely hot, with temperatures often exceeding 45°C.
