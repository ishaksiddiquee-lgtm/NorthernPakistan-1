Discover the "Fruit Garden of Pakistan" in Balochistan

About Quetta

Quetta, the capital of Balochistan province, is located in a high-altitude basin surrounded by mountains. Known as the "Fruit Garden of Pakistan" due to its abundant fruit orchards, especially apples, cherries, and apricots. The city has a strategic location near the Afghanistan border and features historical sites like Hanna Lake and Ziarat.

Quetta is culturally diverse with a mix of Baloch, Pashtun, and other ethnic communities, each contributing to its unique character and traditions. The city serves as an important administrative and commercial center for Balochistan province.

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Quetta is from March to June and September to November when the weather is pleasant. The city enjoys a moderate climate year-round due to its high altitude.
