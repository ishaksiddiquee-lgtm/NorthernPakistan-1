About Badshahi Mosque

Badshahi Mosque is a Mughal-era mosque located in the city of Lahore, capital of the Pakistani province of Punjab. It was commissioned by the sixth Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb in 1671 and completed in 1673. The mosque is made of red sandstone and marble, featuring three domes and four minarets.

The mosque was once the largest mosque in the world and remained so for over 300 years. It covers an area of 400,000 square feet, making it one of the largest mosques in Pakistan and South Asia. The mosque's architecture represents the pinnacle of Mughal architecture.

Visitors to Badshahi Mosque can admire its impressive Mughal architecture, learn about Islamic heritage, and experience the peaceful atmosphere. The mosque is open to non-Muslims during designated hours, offering insight into Mughal history and Islamic culture.

How to Reach Badshahi Mosque

By Air: The nearest airport is Allama Iqbal International Airport in Lahore. From the airport, you can take a taxi or ride-sharing service to reach the mosque (approximately 15-20 minutes).

By Road: Badshahi Mosque is located in the Walled City of Lahore, adjacent to Lahore Fort. It's easily accessible by taxi, rickshaw, or on foot from various parts of the city.

Best Time to Visit: October to March for pleasant weather, early morning or evening hours during summer to avoid heat.

Visitor Information

Opening Hours: Daily from 5:00 AM to 10:00 PM (non-Muslims: 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM)

Entry Fee: Free for all visitors

Dress Code: Modest clothing required; shoulders and knees must be covered. Women should carry a scarf to cover their head.

Guided Tours: Available for groups and individual visitors interested in learning about the mosque's history and architecture.
