About Sukkur

Sukkur is a city in the Sindh province of Pakistan. It is located on the west bank of the Indus River and is known for its historical significance and religious sites. The city is home to the shrine of Hazrat Pir Rakhel Shah, a revered Sufi saint.

Sukkur is an important commercial and agricultural center in Sindh. The city is known for its dates, rice, and other agricultural products. The nearby Kot Diji Fort is an important archaeological site.

Key Attractions

  • Kot Diji Fort
  • Hazrat Pir Rakhel Shah Shrine
  • Indus River Bridge
  • Sukkur Barrage
  • Shah Abdul Latif Bhittai Shrine
  • Thari Park
  • University of Sindh
  • Langah House

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Sukkur is from October to March when the weather is cooler. The summer months can be quite hot.
